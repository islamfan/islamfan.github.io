A joint investigation by the british newspaper Sunday Times and the Bureau of Investigative Journalism has revealed that the US military paid millions of dollars to a PR agency to produce fake videos and attribute them to Al Qaeda during the Iraq war.

The investigation report reveals that the british PR agency 'Bell Pottinger' received half a billion dollars in payments from the Pentagon to manage a secret PR project in Iraq between the years of 2007 and 2011. The agency worked with high ranking US officers in camp victory during a time where Iraq was suffering from unprecedented sectarian violence.

Lord Tim Bell, which served as the chairman of 'Bell Pottinger' at the time, confirmed that his company did work on a secret special operation and sent deliverables to the Pentagon and the CIA. The Sunday Times also reported that 'Bell Pottinger' is known to have worked on PR campaigns for Chilean dictator Augusto Pinochet and for the wife of Syrian president Bashar Assad.

The full report is available at The Bureau of Investigative Journalism website: http://labs.thebureauinvestigates.com/fake-news-and-false-flags/
