Amna Al Haddad just made history: For the first time ever a muslim girl wearing Hijab is featured in Nike's Inner Strength series. Amna is a 26 years old weightlifter from the United Arab Emirates. Here is what she had to say about this opportunity "This is an important moment in history, not for me, but for women empowerment worldwide. Why? To be an athlete from the Middle East, and from the UAE, to be chosen to be featured in Nike recent "inner strength" documentary series as the first woman says a lot about the brand as well as the progress of our country. It is an honor and a big role for me that I am proud of and feel deeply grateful and positive!"

Instagram/Amna Al Haddad

Amna's hard work has earned her 9 medals from competitions in The International Weightlifting Federation. She is also a journalist and motivational speaker.

Instagram/Amna Al Haddad

In the Nike series she talks about her journey and she hopes her work helps improve the lives of other mulsim women. She takes pride in being a Muslim female athlete wearing Hijab.

Instagram/Amna Al Haddad

"You can do whatever you want. Be who you want to be. The most important thing to do is take action. I was not given the tools, nor the circumstances; I just created it and I made it happen."

Amna describes how weightlifting changed her life from a depressed and overweight journalist to a professional athlete full of hope and strength. "From there, I started going to the gym normally and then I got into Cross Fit, which changed my view of strength sports for women, and I just fell in love with weightlifting".
